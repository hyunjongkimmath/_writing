\begin{proposition} \label{proposition:extension_of_scalars_preserves_projective_bimodules_over_rings}
Let $R$ and $S$ be \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{rings}, and let $f: R \to S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{ring homomorphism}. Let $T$ be a ring.

The \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_scalars_for_modules_along_ring_homomorphisms}{base change functor} (extension of scalars) for bimodules,
\[
f^* = S \otimes_R - : {}_R\mathsf{Mod}_T \to {}_S\mathsf{Mod}_T,
\]
\CrefIfExists{definition:category_of_modules_and_bimodules_over_rings}
preserves \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_module}{projective objects}. That is, if $M$ is a projective $R-T$-bimodule, then $S \otimes_R M$ is a projective $S-T$-bimodule.

Similarly, the functor $f^* = - \otimes_R S: {}_T\mathsf{Mod}_R \to {}_T\mathsf{Mod}_S$ sends projective $T-R$-bimodules to projective $T-S$-bimodules.
\end{proposition}

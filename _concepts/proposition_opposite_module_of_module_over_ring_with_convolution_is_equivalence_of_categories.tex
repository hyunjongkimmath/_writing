\begin{proposition} \label{proposition:opposite_module_of_module_over_ring_with_convolution_is_equivalence_of_categories}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperref{definition:involution_on_a_ring}{ring with involution}. 
The opposite module constructions provide an \CrefAndHyperref{definition:equivalence_of_categories}{equivalence of categories} between the \CrefAndHyperref{definition:category_of_modules_and_bimodules_over_rings}{category of left $R$-modules} and the category of right $R$-modules. In fact, if $M$ is a left/right $R$-module, then $(M^{op})^{op}$ is naturally isomorphic to $M$ as a left/right $R$-module via the identity map.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem} \label{theorem:internal_and_external_semidirect_products_are_isomorphic}
Let $G$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group}{group} with \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subgroup_of_a_group}{subgroups} $N$ and $H$.
The group $G$ is the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:semidirect_product_of_groups_external_and_internal}{internal semidirect product} of $N$ and $H$ if and only if $G$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group_homomorphism}{isomorphic} to the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:semidirect_product_of_groups_external_and_internal}{external semidirect product} $N \rtimes_\varphi H$, where the homomorphism $\varphi: H \to \operatorname{Aut}(N)$ is given by \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:conjugation_of_group_elements_and_subgroup_by_a_group_element}{conjugation} within $G$:
$$ \varphi(h)(n) = hnh^{-1} $$
for all $h \in H$ and $n \in N$. Under this isomorphism, the subgroups $N$ and $H$ of the internal product correspond to the subgroups $\{(n, 1) \mid n \in N\}$ and $\{(1, h) \mid h \in H\}$ of the external product, respectively.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[{\cite{roos_dfilr}}, cf. {\cite[Corollary 3.5.4. and the following remark]{weibel}}] \label{theorem:higher_derived_functors_for_an_AB4_star_grothendieck_category_vanish}

Let $\calA$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:abelian_category}{abelian category} satisfying \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendiecks_additional_axioms_for_abelian_categories}{axiom (AB4*)} that is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendiecks_additional_axioms_for_abelian_categories}{Grothendieck category}.
 For any \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:tower_of_objects_in_a_category}{tower} $\{A_i\}$ of objects in $\mathcal{A}$, the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:left_right_derived_functors_of_a_right_left_exact_functor_between_abelian_categories_where_source_has_enough_projectives_injectives}{higher derived functors} of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_and_inductive_limits_in_categories}{inverse limit} vanish for $n \geq 2$, i.e., \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_and_inductive_limits_in_categories}{$R^n \lim A_i = 0$} for all $n \geq 2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
    Roos \cite[Corollaire \'a Proposition 5]{roos_sfdla} had originally stated \Cref{theorem:higher_derived_functors_for_an_AB4_star_grothendieck_category_vanish} for general abelian categories satisying \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendiecks_additional_axioms_for_abelian_categories}{($AB4^*$)} (which are not necessarily \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendiecks_additional_axioms_for_abelian_categories}{Grothendieck}). Neeman \cite{neeman_c1961tha} later produced a counterexample to Roos' statement. Roos later provided a corrected treatment \cite{roos_dfilr}, establishing \Cref{theorem:higher_derived_functors_for_an_AB4_star_grothendieck_category_vanish}. In particular, {\cite[Corollary 3.5.4. and the following remark]{weibel}} is a formulation of Roos' original erred statement.
\end{remark}

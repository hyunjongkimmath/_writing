\begin{theorem}[Category of schemes admits projective limits] \label{theorem:category_of_schemes_over_a_scheme_admits_projective_limits}

    Let $S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}.
    The \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme_over_a_scheme}{category of $S$-schemes} admits all \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_and_inductive_limits_in_categories}{cofiltered limits} whose transition morphisms are \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:affine_morphism_of_schemes}{affine}. 
    More precisely, given any \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:system_in_a_category_indexed_by_a_directed_poset}{cofiltered system} $\{X_i\}_{i \in I}$ of $S$-schemes such that $X_i \to X_j$ is an affine morphism for any arrow $i \in j$ in $I$, the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_and_inductive_limits_in_categories}{inverse limit} $\varprojlim_{i \in I} X_i$ exists.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition} \label{proposition:the_stacks_in_groupoids_over_a_site_form_a_2_category}
Let $(\mathcal{C}, J)$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendieck_topology_on_a_category_site_covering_sieve_topologically_generating_family}{site}. The stacks (in groupoids) over $\mathcal{C}$, together with 1-morphisms (i.e., functors of fibered categories over $\mathcal{C}$) and 2-morphisms (i.e., natural transformations of such functors), form a $2$-category.

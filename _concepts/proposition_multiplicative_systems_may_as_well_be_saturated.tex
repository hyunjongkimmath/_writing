\begin{proposition} \label{proposition:multiplicative_systems_may_as_well_be_saturated}
Let $\mathscr{C}$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:category}{category} and $S$ a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:multiplicative_system_of_morphisms_in_a_category}{multiplicative system}. There exists a unique \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:saturated_multiplicative_system_in_a_category}{saturated multiplicative system} \hl{$\bar{S}$} containing $S$ such that the canonical functors $\mathscr{C}(S^{-1}) \to \mathscr{C}(\bar{S}^{-1})$ define an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:equivalence_of_categories}{equivalence of categories}. $\bar{S}$ consists of all morphisms $f$ such that there exist morphisms $g, h$ with $g \circ f \in S$ and $f \circ h \in S$.
\end{proposition}

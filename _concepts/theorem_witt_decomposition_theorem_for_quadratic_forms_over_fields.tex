\begin{theorem}[Witt decomposition theorem] \label{theorem:witt_decomposition_theorem_for_quadratic_forms_over_fields}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field}{field} and $q: M \to R$ a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:nondegenerate_quadratic_form_on_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring}{non-degenerate} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quadratic_formm_on_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring}{quadratic form}. 
Then there exists a unique (up to isomorphism) decomposition $M \cong M_a \oplus H^k$ where $M_a$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:isotropic_vector_subspace_anisotropic_quadratic_form_of_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring_with_quadratic_form}{anisotropic} and $H^k$ is a direct sum of $k$ \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:hyperbolic_plane_over_a_commutative_ring}{hyperbolic planes}.

The integer $k$ is called the \hldef{\emph{Witt index} of $q$}.
\end{theorem}

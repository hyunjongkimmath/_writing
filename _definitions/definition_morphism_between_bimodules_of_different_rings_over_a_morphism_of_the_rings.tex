\begin{definition} \label{definition:morphism_between_bimodules_of_different_rings_over_a_morphism_of_the_rings}
Let $\mathcal{R}ing$ be the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:category_of_rings}{category of (associative, with identity) rings}. Let $(R, S)$ and $(R', S')$ be objects in \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:product_category_of_a_family_of_categories}{$\mathcal{R}ing^2$}. Let $M$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_of_a_ring}{$(R, S)$-bimodule} and $M'$ be an $(R', S')$-bimodule. 
A \hldef{bimodule morphism over a morphism $(f, g) \in \text{Hom}_{\mathcal{R}ing^2}((R, S), (R', S'))$} is a group homomorphism $\phi: M \to M'$ such that for all $r \in R, s \in S, m \in M$:
$$\phi(r \cdot m \cdot s) = f(r) \cdot \phi(m) \cdot g(s)$$
\end{definition}

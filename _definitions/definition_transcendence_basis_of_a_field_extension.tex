\begin{definition}[Transcendence basis] \label{definition:transcendence_basis_of_a_field_extension}
Let $E/F$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension}. A subset $T \subseteq E$ is called a \hldef{transcendence basis} of $E$ over $F$ if:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item $T$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{TODO}{algebraically independent} over $F$, i.e., there is no non-trivial polynomial relation with coefficients in $F$ among finitely many elements of $T$, and
    
    \item $E$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraic_element_minimal_polynomial_of_an_algebraic_element_algebraic_field_extension_transcendental_element_field_extension}{algebraic} over $F(T)$, where $F(T)$ denotes the field \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field_generated_by_elements}{generated by} $F$ and $T$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

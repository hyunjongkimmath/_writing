\begin{definition} \label{definition:relative_spectrum_of_a_quasi_coherent_sheaf_of_O_algebras_on_a_scheme}
Let $S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme} and let $\mathcal{A}$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_general_scheme}{quasi-coherent sheaf} of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebra_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{$\mathcal{O}_S$-algebras}. The \hldef{relative spectrum of $\mathcal{A}$}, denoted by notations such as \hl{$\mathbf{Spec}(\mathcal{A})$}, \hl{$\mathcal{S}pec(\mathcal{A})$}, or \hl{$\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}(\calA)$}, is the unique $S$-scheme $f: X \to S$ such that for every open subset $U \subseteq S$, there is a natural isomorphism
$$\operatorname{Hom}_{\text{Sch}/U}(T, f^{-1}(U)) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{O}_S(U)\text{-alg}}(\mathcal{A}(U), \Gamma(T, \mathcal{O}_T))$$
\CrefIfExists{definition:preimage_of_an_open_subset_of_a_scheme_under_a_morphism_of_schemes}\CrefIfExists{definition:presheaf_on_a_topological_space}
for any scheme $T$ over $U$. Affine locally, if $S = \operatorname{Spec} R$\CrefIfExists{affine_scheme} and $\mathcal{A} = \widetilde{A}$\CrefIfExists{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_an_affine_scheme}, then $\mathbf{Spec}(\mathcal{A}) \cong \operatorname{Spec} A$.
\end{definition}

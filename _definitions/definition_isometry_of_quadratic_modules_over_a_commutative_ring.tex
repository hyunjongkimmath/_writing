\begin{definition} \label{definition:isometry_of_quadratic_modules_over_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring}, and let $M$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quadratic_formm_on_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring}{quadratic module}.

An \emph{isometry} $\phi: M \to M'$ is an $R$-module isomorphism such that $q'( \phi(m) ) = q(m)$ for all $m \in M$.

Two quadratic modules $(M,q)$ and $(M',q')$ are \emph{isometric}, written $(M,q) \cong (M',q')$, if there exists an isometry $\phi: M \to M'$.

\TextIfExists{definition:isometry_of_quadratic_modules_over_an_involution_ring}{Equivalently, these notions can be defiend via \Cref{definition:isometry_of_quadratic_modules_over_an_involution_ring} where $R$ is considered as an involution ring with the identity morphism.}
\end{definition}

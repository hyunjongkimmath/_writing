\begin{definition} \label{definition:integral_scheme}
Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}. We say that $X$ is an \hldef{integral scheme} if $X$ is both a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:reduced_scheme}{reduced scheme} and an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:irreducible_topological_space}{irreducible topological space}. Equivalently, $X$ is integral if for every non-empty open subset $U \subseteq X$, the ring $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:zero_divisor_of_a_ring}{integral domain}.
\end{definition}

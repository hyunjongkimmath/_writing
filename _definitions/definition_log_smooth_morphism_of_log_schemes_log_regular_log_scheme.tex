\begin{definition} \label{definition:log_smooth_morphism_of_log_schemes_log_regular_log_scheme}

    Let $(X, M_X)$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:pre_logarithmic_structure_and_logarithmic_structure_on_a_scheme_and_log_scheme}{log scheme}. A \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:morphism_of_log_schemes}{morphism} $f: (X,M_X) \to (Y,M_Y)$ of log schemes is \hldef{log smooth} if it is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:smooth_morphism_of_schemes}{smooth} as a morphism of schemes and étale locally admits charts inducing smooth morphisms of monoids. A log scheme is \hldef{log regular} if it is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:integral_scheme}{integral}, \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:fine_toric_scheme_over_a_scheme}{fine}, \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:saturated_toric_scheme_over_a_scheme}{saturated} and locally isomorphic (in the étale topology) to \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:toric_variety_over_a_field}{toric varieties} with their standard log structure.  
\end{definition}

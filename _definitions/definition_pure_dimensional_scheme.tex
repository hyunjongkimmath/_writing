\begin{definition} \label{definition:pure_dimensional_scheme}
Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}.

We say that $X$ is \hldef{pure dimensional} (or \hldef{equidimensional}) if every irreducible component of $X$ has the same dimension. That is, if $X = \bigcup_{i \in I} Z_i$ is the decomposition of $X$ into its irreducible components, there exists an integer $d$ such that:
$$\dim(Z_i) = d \quad \text{for all } i \in I$$
where \hl{$\dim(Z_i)$} denotes the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:dimension_of_a_topological_space}{Krull dimension} of the topological space $Z_i$.

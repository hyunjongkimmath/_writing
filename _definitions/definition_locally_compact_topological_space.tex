\begin{definition}[Locally compact] \label{definition:locally_compact_topological_space}
Let $(X, \mathcal{T})$ be a \hyperrefIfExists{definition:topological_space}{topological space}. $X$ is \hldef{locally compact} if for every $x \in X$, there exists an open set $U \in \mathcal{T}$ containing $x$ and a \hyperrefIfExists{definition:compact_topological_space}{compact set} $K \subseteq X$ such that $x \in U \subseteq K$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:irrelevant_ideal_of_a_graded_ring_by_a_monoid_with_an_absorbing_zero_or_ordered_monoid_with_zero_degre_the_identity}
Let $\Gamma$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:monoid}{monoid} with an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:absorbing_zero_of_a_monoid}{absorbing zero} $0$ (or let $\Gamma$ be an ordered monoid with identity $e$). Let $R = \bigoplus_{\gamma \in \Gamma} R_\gamma$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:graded_ring_by_a_monoid}{$\Gamma$-graded ring}. The \hldef{irrelevant ideal of $R$}, denoted by \hl{$R_+$}, is the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ideal_of_a_ring}{ideal} generated by all \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:graded_ring_by_a_monoid}{homogeneous elements} of "positive" degree (i.e., degrees not equal to the neutral element of the grading monoid). Explicitly:
$$R_+ = \bigoplus_{\gamma \in \Gamma \setminus \{e\}} R_\gamma.$$
(If $\Gamma$ is additive with identity $0$, then $R_+ = \bigoplus_{\gamma \neq 0} R_\gamma$).
In particular, we may speak of a the irrelevant ideal of a $\bbZ_{\geq 0}$-graded ring.
\end{definition}

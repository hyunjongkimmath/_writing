\begin{definition}[Cauchy sequence in an extended metric space] \label{definition:cauchy_sequence_in_an_extended_metric_space}
Let $(M, d)$ be an \hyperrefIfExists{definition:extended_metric_on_a_set}{extended metric space}. A sequence $(x_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ in $M$ is called a \hldef{Cauchy sequence} if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for all $m, n \geq N$, $d(x_m, x_n) < \epsilon$ (and thus, in particular, all pairwise distances $d(x_m, x_n)$ are finite for $m, n \geq N$).
\end{definition}

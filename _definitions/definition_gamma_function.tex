\begin{definition} \label{definition:gamma_function}
The \hldef{Gamma function} \hl{$\Gamma : \{ z \in \mathbb{C} : \Re(z) > 0 \} \to \mathbb{C}$} is defined by the integral
$$
\Gamma(z) := \int_0^\infty t^{z-1} e^{-t} \, dt,
$$
which converges absolutely for all complex numbers $z$ with real part $\Re(z) > 0$.

The Gamma function $\Gamma(z)$ admits a unique meromorphic continuation to the entire complex plane $\mathbb{C}$, with simple poles at non-positive integers $z = 0, -1, -2, \ldots$, and satisfies the functional equation
$$ \Gamma(z+1) = z \Gamma(z).  $$
\end{definition}

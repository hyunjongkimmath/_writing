\begin{definition} \label{definition:twisting_sheaf_of_serre_of_degree_n_on_projective_space_over_a_scheme}
Let $S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme} and let $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{O}_S[T_0, \dots, T_m]$ be the sheaf of polynomial algebras defining the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_space_of_dimension_n_over_a_scheme}{projective space} $\mathbb{P}^m_S = \mathbf{Proj}(\mathcal{A})$.

The \hldef{twisting sheaf of Serre of degree $n$} is the quasi-coherent sheaf \hl{$\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^m_S}(n)$}\CrefIfExists{definition:shift_of_a_quasi_coherent_graded_sheaf_of_algebras_on_a_scheme} on $\mathbb{P}^m_S$ associated to the graded $\mathcal{A}$-module $\mathcal{A}(n)$.

Explicitly, on the standard affine open cover $U_i = D_+(T_i) \cong \mathbb{A}^m_S$ (for $i=0, \dots, m$), the sheaf is trivial and generated by $T_i^n$. The sections over an open set $V \subseteq U_i$ are given by:
$$\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^m_S}(n)(V) = \left\{ \frac{f}{T_i^d} \in \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^m_S}(V) \cdot T_i^n \right\}.$$

The transition function between the charts $U_i$ and $U_j$ is multiplication by $(T_j/T_i)^n$. This sheaf is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraic_vector_bundle_over_a_scheme}{invertible sheaf} (a line bundle) and is called the \hldef{line bundle of degree $n$}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:shift_of_a_quasi_coherent_graded_sheaf_of_algebras_on_a_scheme}
Let $S$ be a scheme and let $\mathcal{A} = \bigoplus_{d \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathcal{A}_d$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_general_scheme}{quasi-coherent graded sheaf} of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{$\mathcal{O}_S$-algebras}. Let $\mathcal{M} = \bigoplus_{d \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathcal{M}_d$ be a graded $\mathcal{A}$-module. For any integer $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, the \hldef{$n$-th shifted graded module of $\mathcal{M}$}, denoted by \hl{$\mathcal{M}(n)$}, is the graded $\mathcal{A}$-module defined by shifting the grading of $\mathcal{M}$ such that the degree $d$ component is given by:
$$\hlin{\mathcal{M}(n)_d = \mathcal{M}_{n+d}.}$$
The module structure is compatible with the shift: if $a \in \mathcal{A}_k$ and $m \in \mathcal{M}(n)_d = \mathcal{M}_{n+d}$, then the product $a \cdot m$ lies in $\mathcal{M}_{n+d+k} = \mathcal{M}(n)_{d+k}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Accumulation point in an extended metric space] \label{definition:accumulation_point_in_an_extended_metric_space}
Let $(X,d)$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extended_metric_on_a_set}{extended metric space} and let $A \subseteq X$ be a subset.

A point $x \in X$ is called an \hldef{accumulation point of $A$ (with respect to $d$} if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ with $\varepsilon \in (0,\infty)$ there exists a point $y \in A \setminus \{x\}$ such that $d(x,y) < \varepsilon$.
Equivalently, $x$ is an accumulation point of $A$ if every open ball $B_d(x,\varepsilon) = \{y \in X : d(x,y) < \varepsilon\}$ with $\varepsilon \in (0,\infty)$ contains a point of $A$ distinct from $x$.
\end{definition}

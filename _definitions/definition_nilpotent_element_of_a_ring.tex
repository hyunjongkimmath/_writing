\begin{definition} \label{definition:nilpotent_element_of_a_ring}
Let $R$ be an associative \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{ring with identity}, not necessarily commutative. An element $x \in R$ is called a \hldef{nilpotent element} if there exists a positive integer $n \geq 1$ such that $x^n = 0$.
\end{definition}

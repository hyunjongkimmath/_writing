\begin{definition} \label{definition:induced_ring_homomorphism_of_stalks_for_a_morphism_of_ringed_spaces}
    Let $(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ and $(Y, \mathcal{O}_Y)$ be \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ringed_space}{ringed spaces}. Suppose that $f: (X, \calO_X) \to (Y, \calO_Y)$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:morphism_of_ringed_spaces}{morphism of ringed spaces}. By \Cref{definition:morphism_induced_on_stalks_by_a_morphism_of_presheaves_on_a_topological_space_valued_in_a_category_with_direct_limits} there is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{ring homomorphism} 
    $$f_x^\#: \calO_{Y,f(x)} \to \calO_{X,x}$$
    of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:stalk_of_a_presheaf_on_a_topological_space_at_a_point}{stalks}. This may be referred to the \hldef{ring homomorphism induced by $f$ on the stalks}.
\end{definition}

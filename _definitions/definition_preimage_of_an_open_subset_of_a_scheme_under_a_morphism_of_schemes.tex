
\begin{definition} \label{definition:preimage_of_an_open_subset_of_a_scheme_under_a_morphism_of_schemes}
Let $X$ and $Y$ be \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{schemes}, and let 
$$f : X \to Y$$ 
be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:morphism_of_schemes}{morphism of schemes}. For an open subset $V \subseteq Y$, the \hldef{preimage of $V$ under $f$} is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:preimage_of_an_open_subset_of_a_locally_ringed_spaces_under_a_morphism_of_locally_ringed_spaces}{preimage of $V$ under $f$} as a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:morphism_of_locally_ringed_spaces}{morphism of locally ringed spaces}:
$$\hlin{f^{-1}(V) := \{ x \in X \mid f(x) \in V \} \subseteq X.}$$
This preimage is always an open subscheme of $X$ with the structure sheaf inherited from $\mathcal{O}_X$, and is called the \hldef{scheme-theoretic preimage of $V$ under $f$}.
\end{definition}

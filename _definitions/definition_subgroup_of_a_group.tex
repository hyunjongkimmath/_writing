
\begin{definition} \label{definition:subgroup_of_a_group}
Let $(G,\cdot)$ be a group. A subset $H \subseteq G$ is called a \hldef{subgroup of $G$} if $H$, with the operation induced from $G$, is a group. That is, $H$ is a subgroup if:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $H \neq \emptyset$,
    \item For all $h_1,h_2 \in H$, the product $h_1 \cdot h_2 \in H$,
    \item For all $h \in H$, the inverse $h^{-1} \in H$.
\end{itemize}
We denote this relation by \hl{$H \leq G$}, meaning ``$H$ is a subgroup of $G$''.
\end{definition}

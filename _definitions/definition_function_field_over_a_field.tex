\begin{definition}[Function field] \label{definition:function_field_over_a_field}
Let $k$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field}{field}. 
A field $K$ is called a \hldef{function field over $k$} (or a \hldef{function field in $n$ variables over $k$}) if $K$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:finitely_generated_field_extension}{finitely generated field extension} of $k$ with \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:transcendence_degree_of_a_field_extension}{transcendence degree} $n$ over $k$, where $n \geq 1$. Equivalently, the function field in $n$ variables over $k$ is isomorphic to the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field_of_fractions_of_an_integral_domain}{fraction field} of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:polynomial_ring_over_a_commutative_ring}{polynomial ring} $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ where $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ are indeterminate variables. Accordingly, the function field in $n$ variables over $k$ is often denoted as \hl{$k(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$}.

When $n = 1$, we say that $K$ is a \hldef{function field of one variable over $k$} or an \hldef{algebraic function field over $k$} and denote it by \hl{$k(x)$}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_ringed_space}
Let $(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ringed_space}{ringed space}. A \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules} $\mathcal{F}$ is \hldef{quasi-coherent} if for every point $x \in X$, there exists an open neighborhood $U \subseteq X$ of $x$ and an exact sequence of $\mathcal{O}_U$-modules
$$ \mathcal{O}_U^{(I)} \to \mathcal{O}_U^{(J)} \to \mathcal{F}|_U \to 0, $$
where $I$ and $J$ are index sets (possibly infinite), and $\mathcal{O}_U^{(I)}, \mathcal{O}_U^{(J)}$ denote direct sums of copies of the structure sheaf.

\TextIfExists{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_ringed_site}{
Equivalently, a quasi-coherent sheaf on the ringed space $(X, \calO_X)$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_ringed_site}{quasi-coherent sheaf} on $(X, \calO_X)$ \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ringed_space}{as a} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ringed_site}{ringed site}. 
}
\end{definition}

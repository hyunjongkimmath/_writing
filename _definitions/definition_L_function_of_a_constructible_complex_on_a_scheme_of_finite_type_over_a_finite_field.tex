
\begin{definition}[L-function of a constructible complex] \label{definition:L_function_of_a_constructible_complex_on_a_scheme_of_finite_type_over_a_finite_field}
Let \(X_0\) be a scheme of finite type over the finite field \(\mathbb{F}_q\), let $\ell \neq \Char \Fq$ be a prime, and let \(K_0 \in D^b_c(X_0, \overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell)\).

The \hldef{L-function of $K_0$} is the following Euler product over all closed points of \(X_0\):
$$\hlin{L(X_0, K_0, t) := \prod_{x \in |X_0|} L_x(K_0, t) = \prod_{x \in |X_0|} \det\left( 1 - t^{d(x)} \,\mathrm{Frob}_x \mid H^*(K_{0, \bar{x}}) \right)^{-1}. }$$
We shall call such an $L$-function a \hldef{Grothendieck $L$-function}, in deference to Grothendieck's theory of this $L$-function\TextIfExists{theorem:grothendieck_L_function_of_a_complex_is_a_determinant_of_the_total_compactly_supported_cohomology_of_the_complex}{ (e.g. see \Cref{theorem:grothendieck_L_function_of_a_complex_is_a_determinant_of_the_total_compactly_supported_cohomology_of_the_complex})}.
\end{definition}

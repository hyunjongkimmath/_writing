\begin{definition} \label{definition:characteristic_of_a_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{ring}.
There exists a unique \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{ring homomorphism} $\psi: \mathbb{Z} \to R$ defined by mapping $n \mapsto n \cdot 1_K$. The non-negative generator of the kernel of this map, $\ker(\psi) = \langle p \rangle \subseteq \mathbb{Z}$, is called the \hldef{characteristic of $R$}, denoted by \hl{$\operatorname{char}(R)$}.
\end{definition}

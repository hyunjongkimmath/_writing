\begin{definition} \label{definition:central_algebra_over_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring} and let $(A, \varphi)$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebra_of_a_ring}{$R$-algebra}. The algebra $A$ is called a \hldef{central $R$-algebra} if the structure map $\varphi: R \to Z(A)$ induces an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{isomorphism} between $R$ and the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:center_of_a_ring}{center of $A$}. Specifically, this requires two conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The map $\varphi$ is surjective onto $Z(A)$, meaning $Z(A) = \varphi(R)$.
    \item The map $\varphi$ is injective (faithful), so $R \cong \varphi(R)$.
\end{enumerate}
In other words, $A$ is central if $Z(A) \cong R$ via the structure map.
\end{definition}

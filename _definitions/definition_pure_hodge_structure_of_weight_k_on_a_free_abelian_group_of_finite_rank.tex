
\begin{definition} \label{definition:pure_hodge_structure_of_weight_k_on_a_free_abelian_group}
Let $L$ be a free abelian group of finite rank, and let $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ be an integer. 
A \hldef{(pure) Hodge structure of weight $k$ on $L$} is a decomposition
$$L_{\mathbb{C}} = \bigoplus_{p+q = k} L^{p,q}$$
of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:complexification_of_a_free_abelian_group_of_finite_rank}{complexification} $L_\bbC$ into complex subspaces \hl{$L^{p,q}$} (called the \hldef{Hodge components}) such that 
$$ \overline{L^{p,q}} = L^{q,p}, $$

The pair $(p,q)$ is referred to as the \hldef{bidegree of the component $L^{p,q}$}.
\end{definition}

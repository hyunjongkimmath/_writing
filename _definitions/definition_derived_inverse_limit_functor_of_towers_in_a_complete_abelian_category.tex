\begin{definition} \label{definition:derived_inverse_limit_functor_of_towers_in_a_complete_abelian_category}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:abelian_category}{abelian category} with \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:has_enough_injectives_or_projectives_for_an_abelian_category}{enough injectives}. Let $I$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:locally_small_category}{small category}.
% the poset of non-negative integers $\mathbb{N}$ with the reverse order $\cdots \rightarrow 2 \rightarrow 1 \rightarrow 0$, so that \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:diagram_in_a_category_indexed_by_a_small_category}{$\calA^I$} is the category of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:tower_of_objects_in_a_category}{towers} in $\calA$.
Assume that $\calA$ admits all limits indexed by $I$. 
\TODO{comment on how $\calA^I$ has enough injectives}

For $n \geq 0$, the \hldef{$n$th derived inverse limit functor} is the right derived functor $R^n \lim: \mathcal{A}^I \to \mathcal{A}$, also denoted \hl{$\lim^n A_i$},  of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_and_inductive_limits_in_categories}{inverse limit functor} $\lim: \mathcal{A}^I \rightarrow \mathcal{A}$. Note that a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{theorem:limit_and_colimit_are_left_right_adjoint_to_diagonal_functor_for_locally_small_base_and_small_index}{limit functor} (of diagrams indexed by small categories on $\calA$) is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:exact_functor_between_abelian_categories}{left exact} because it is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:adjoint_functors_between_categories_unit_counit_of_adjoint_functors}{right adjoint} to a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:diagonal_functor_from_a_category_to_a_diagram_category_of_the_category}{diagonal functor} (\Cref{proposition:left_right_adjoint_is_right_left_exact}) (\Cref{theorem:limit_and_colimit_are_left_right_adjoint_to_diagonal_functor_for_locally_small_base_and_small_index}).
% The $n$-th derived functors of the inverse limit are denoted by \hl{$R^n \lim: \mathcal{A}^I \rightarrow \mathcal{A}$}. 
\end{definition}

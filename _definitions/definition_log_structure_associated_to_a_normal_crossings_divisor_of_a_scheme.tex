\begin{definition} \label{definition:log_structure_associated_to_a_normal_crossings_divisor_of_a_scheme}
Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}. 
Let $j: U \hookrightarrow X$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:open_and_closed_immersions_of_schemes}{open immersion} such that the complement $D = X \setminus U$ is a divisor with \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:normal_crossings_effective_Q_divisor_on_a_regular_scheme}{normal crossings}. 
The \hldef{standard log structure associated to the pair $(X, D)$} is the sheaf of monoids $M$ defined by
$$\hlin{M = \{f \in \mathcal{O}_X \mid f|_U \in \mathcal{O}_U^* \} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_X,}$$
with the structure map $\alpha: M \hookrightarrow \mathcal{O}_X$ being the inclusion. This is often called the \hldef{log structure defined by the divisor $D$}.

\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_ringed_site}
Let $(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{O})$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ringed_site}{ringed site}. An \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{$\mathcal{O}$-module sheaf} $\mathcal{F}$ is \hldef{quasi-coherent} if there exists a covering $\{U_i\}$ in $\mathcal{C}$ such that for each $i$, there is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:acyclic_complex_of_objects_in_an_abelian_category}{exact sequence} of $\mathcal{O}|_{U_i}$-modules
$$ \mathcal{O}_{U_i}^{(I_i)} \to \mathcal{O}_{U_i}^{(J_i)} \to \mathcal{F}|_{U_i} \to 0, $$
with $I_i, J_i$ index sets.
\end{definition}

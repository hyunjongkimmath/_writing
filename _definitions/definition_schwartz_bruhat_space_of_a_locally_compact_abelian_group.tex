\begin{definition}[Schwartz-Bruhat space on a locally compact abelian group] \label{definition:schwartz_bruhat_space_of_a_locally_compact_abelian_group}
Let $G$ be a \hyperrefIfExists{definition:locally_compact_group}{locally compact abelian group}\CrefIfExists{definition:locally_compact_group} that is a finite product of copies of $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{Z}$, and finite groups, or more generally a locally compact abelian group admitting a smooth structure and \hyperrefIfExists{definition:haar_measure_on_a_locally_compact_group}{Haar measure}\CrefIfExists{definition:haar_measure_on_a_locally_compact_group}. The \hldef{Schwartz-Bruhat space} \hl{$\mathcal{S}(G)$} is the space of functions which are analogues of Schwartz functions on $G$: smooth, rapidly decreasing if archimedean, and locally constant with compact support if non-archimedean. More precisely:
\begin{itemize}
    \item If $G = \mathbb{R}^n$, then $\mathcal{S}(G)$  is the classical \hyperrefIfExists{definition:schwartz_space_on_Rn}{Schwartz space $\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^n)$}\CrefIfExists{definition:schwartz_space_on_Rn} on $\bbR^n$.
    \item If $G$ is a non-archimedean local field (or its additive group), then $\mathcal{S}(G)$ is the space of locally constant, compactly supported complex-valued functions $G \to \bbC$.

    \item For a finite product of such groups, the Schwartz-Bruhat space is the completed tensor product of the corresponding spaces.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

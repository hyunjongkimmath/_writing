\begin{definition} \label{definition:Q_divisor_on_a_scheme}
Let $X$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}. A \hldef{$\mathbb{Q}$-divisor on $X$} is a formal linear combination $D = \sum q_i Y_i$, where the $Y_i$ are prime divisors and the coefficients $q_i$ are rational numbers, with only finitely many $q_i$ non-zero. 
\end{definition}

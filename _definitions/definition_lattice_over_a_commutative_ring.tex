
\begin{definition} \label{defintion:lattice_over_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring} and let $M$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_of_a_ring}{$R$-module}. A \hldef{lattice} (or \hldef{$R$-lattice}) is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:submodule_of_a_module_over_a_ring}{submodule} $L \subseteq M$ which is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:finitely_generated_modules_over_rings}{finitely generated} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:injective_and_projective_objects_in_a_category}{projective} $R$-module. 

In the specific case where $R$ is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:zero_divisor_of_a_ring}{integral domain} with \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field_of_fractions_of_an_integral_domain}{field of fractions} $K$ and $V$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:basis_of_a_vector_space_over_a_field}{finite-dimensional} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:vector_space_over_a_field}{$K$-vector space}, an \hldef{$R$-lattice in $V$} is a finitely generated $R$-submodule $L \subset V$ such that $K \cdot L = V$ (i.e., $L$ spans $V$ over $K$).

Given an $R$-lattice $N$, and an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{extension} $L$ of $K$, we often let \hl{$N_L$} denote the $L$-vector space \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:tensor_product_of_bimodules_of_rings}{$N \otimes_R L$}. 

A \hldef{lattice}, without reference to a base ring, usually refers to a $\bbZ$-lattice, i.e. a free abelian group of finite rank, or equivalently a discrete subgroup of a real vector space of the same dimension that spans the space. As above, we let \hl{$N_\mathbb{R}$} denote the $\mathbb{R}$-vector space $N \otimes_\mathbb{Z} \mathbb{R}$. 
\end{definition}

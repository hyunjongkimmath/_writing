\begin{definition} \label{definition:compatible_with_triangulation_for_a_multiplicative_system_of_a_triangulated_category}
In the context of a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:triangulated_category}{triangulated category} $\mathscr{D}$, a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:multiplicative_system_of_morphisms_in_a_category}{multiplicative system} $S$ is \hldef{compatible with the triangulation} if:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item For any $s \in S$, $T(s) \in S$ where $T$ is the translation functor.
    \item For any commutative square between the first two steps of two distinguished triangles where the vertical arrows are in $S$, there exists a morphism in $S$ between the third objects that makes the whole diagram of triangles commute.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:symmetric_group_on_a_set}
Let $X$ be a set. The \hldef{symmetric group on $X$} or the \hldef{permutation group on $X$}, denoted by \hl{$\mathfrak{S}_X$}, \hl{$S_X$}, or \hl{$\Sym_X$}, is the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group}{group} whose elements are all \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:injective_surjective_bijective_map_of_sets}{bijections} $\sigma : X \to X$, with the group operation being composition of maps:
$$ (\sigma \circ \tau)(x) := \sigma(\tau(x)), \quad \sigma, \tau \in \mathfrak{S}_X, \; x \in X.  $$
The identity element is the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:identity_function_on_a_set}{identity map $\mathrm{id}_X$}, and the inverse of $\sigma$ is its \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:injective_surjective_bijective_map_of_sets}{inverse function $\sigma^{-1}$}.

Elements of the symmetric group are called \hldef{permutations}.

A symmetric group on a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:countable_finite_uncountable_sets}{finite set} of $n$ elements is typically denoted by \hl{$\mathfrak{S}_n$}, \hldef{$S_n$}, or \hldef{$\Sym_n$}. Any two symmetric groups on sets of equal cardinality are \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group_homomorphism}{isomorphic}.
\end{definition}

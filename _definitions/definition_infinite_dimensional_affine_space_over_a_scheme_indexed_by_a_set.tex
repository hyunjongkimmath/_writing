\begin{definition}[Infinite dimensional affine space] \label{definition:infinite_dimensional_affine_space_over_a_scheme_indexed_by_a_set}
Let $S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:affine_scheme}{scheme} and let $I$ be an arbitrary index set.
The \hldef{affine space over $S$ indexed by $I$}, denoted \hl{$\mathbb{A}^I_S$} or \hl{$\mathbb{A}^{(I)}_S$}, is defined as the scheme
$$\hlin{\mathbb{A}^I_S = \underline{\operatorname{Spec}} \mathcal{O}_S[x_i \mid i \in I] = S \times_{\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}} \operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}[x_i \mid i \in I],}$$
\CrefIfExists{definition:relative_spectrum_of_a_quasi_coherent_sheaf_of_O_algebras_on_a_scheme}
where $\mathcal{O}_S[x_i \mid i \in I]$ denotes the symmetric algebra of the free $\mathcal{O}_S$-module with basis indexed by $I$. Equivalently, $\mathbb{A}^I_S$ represents the functor that assigns to each $S$-scheme $T$ the set of families $(f_i)_{i \in I}$ of global sections $f_i \in \Gamma(T, \mathcal{O}_T)$. When $I = \mathbb{N}$ or $I$ is countably infinite, we may write \hl{$\mathbb{A}^\infty_S$} for the infinite dimensional affine space over $S$.
\end{definition}

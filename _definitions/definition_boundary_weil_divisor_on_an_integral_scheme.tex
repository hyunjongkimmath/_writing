\begin{definition} \label{definition:boundary_weil_divisor_on_an_integral_scheme}
Let $X$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:integral_scheme}{integral scheme}. A \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:prime_and_weil_divisor_on_a_scheme}{Weil divisor} is said to be a \hldef{boundary divisor} if it is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:effective_weil_divisor_on_a_scheme}{effective divisor} with coefficients in $[0,1]$.  
\end{definition}

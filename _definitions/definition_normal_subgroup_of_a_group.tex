
\begin{definition} \label{definition:normal_subgroup_of_a_group}
Let $(G,\cdot)$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group}{group}. A \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subgroup_of_a_group}{subgroup} $N \leq G$ is called a \hldef{normal subgroup of $G$} if $N$ is closed under \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:conjugation_of_group_elements_and_subgroup_by_a_group_element}{conjugation} by elements of $G$, i.e. for all $g \in G$ and $n \in N$, one has
$$ gng^{-1} \in N. $$
Equivalently, $N$ is normal in $G$ if every \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:left_and_right_group_actions_on_a_set}{left coset of $N$ in $G$ is also a right coset of $N$ in $G$}.  
We denote this relation by \hl{$N \trianglelefteq G$}, meaning ``$N$ is a normal subgroup of $G$''.
\end{definition}


\begin{definition}[Idelic norm of a global field] \label{definition:idelic_norm_of_the_ideles_of_a_global_field}
Let $F$ be a global field. The \hldef{idelic norm} (also called the \hldef{module}) is the map
$$\hlin{|\cdot| : \mathbb{A}_F^\times \to \mathbb{R}_{>0}}$$
defined by

\[
|(x_{\mathfrak{p}})_{\mathfrak{p}}| \;:=\; \prod_{\mathfrak{p}} |x_{\mathfrak{p}}|_{\mathfrak{p}},
\]
where the product is well-defined as all but finitely many factors equal 1.

By the product formula for global fields, the idelic norm factors through the idele class group and satisfies
\[
|a| = 1 \quad \text{for all } a \in F^\times,
\]
where $F^\times$ is diagonally embedded in $\mathbb{A}_F^\times$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:algebraic_vector_bundle_over_a_scheme}
Let $X$ be a scheme. An \hldef{algebraic vector bundle} (or simply a \hldef{vector bundle}) of rank $n$ on $X$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:free_and_locally_free_modules_on_a_ringed_site}{locally free sheaf} of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{$\mathcal{O}_X$-modules} $\mathcal{E}$ of constant rank $n$. That is, $\mathcal{E}$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:coherent_sheaf_on_a_scheme}{coherent sheaf} of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules such that $X$ can be covered by open sets $U_i$ where the restriction $\mathcal{E}|_{U_i}$ is isomorphic to the free sheaf $\mathcal{O}_{U_i}^{\oplus n}$ for each $i$.

A \hldef{(algebraic) line bundle} or \hldef{invertible sheaf} or is then an algebraic vector bundle of rank $1$.
\end{definition}

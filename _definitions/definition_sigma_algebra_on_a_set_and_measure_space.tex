\begin{definition}[Sigma-algebra and measure] \label{definition:sigma_algebra_on_a_set_and_measure_space}
Let $X$ be a set.
A collection $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of $X$ is called a \hldef{$\sigma$-algebra on $X$} if:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\varnothing \in \mathcal{A}$ and $X \in \mathcal{A}$,
  \item if $A \in \mathcal{A}$ then its complement $X \setminus A \in \mathcal{A}$,
  \item if $(A_n)_{n\ge 1}$ is a sequence with each $A_n \in \mathcal{A}$, then $\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n \in \mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
A pair $(X,\mathcal{A})$ is called a \hldef{measurable space}.

\end{definition}

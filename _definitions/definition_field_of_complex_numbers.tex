\begin{definition}[Field of complex numbers] \label{definition:field_of_complex_numbers}
    A \hldef{field of complex numbers} is a field $\mathbb{C}$ together with an injective field homomorphism $\iota : \mathbb{R} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{C}$ from the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field_of_real_numbers}{field of real numbers} such that:
    \begin{enumerate}
    \item $\mathbb{C}$ is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraically_closure_of_a_field}{algebraic closure} of $\iota(\mathbb{R})$ (every nonconstant polynomial with coefficients in $\iota(\mathbb{R})$ has a root in $\mathbb{C}$, and $\mathbb{C}$ is algebraic over $\iota(\mathbb{R})$),
    \item $\mathbb{C}$ has characteristic $0$.
    \end{enumerate}
    Such a pair $(\mathbb{C},\iota)$ is unique up to unique isomorphism of fields over $\mathbb{R}$.
    We write \hl{$\mathbb{C}$} for any chosen field of complex numbers together with the implied embedding of $\mathbb{R}$.

    In fact, $\bbC$ can be identified by \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subalgebra_of_an_algebra_over_a_not_necessarily_commutative_ring_generated_by_a_subset}{$\bbR[i]$} where $i$ is an element of $\bbC$ with relation $i^2 = -1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:proper_morphism_of_schemes}
Let $f: X \to Y$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:morphism_of_schemes}{morphism of schemes}. The morphism $f$ is said to be \hldef{proper} if it satisfies the following three conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $f$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quasi_separated_morphism_of_schemes}{separated},
    \item $f$ is of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:finite_type_morphism_of_schemes}{finite type},
    \item $f$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:universally_closed_morphism_of_schemes}{universally closed}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

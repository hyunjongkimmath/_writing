\begin{definition} \label{definition:homogeneous_ideal_of_a_graded_ring_by_a_monoid}
Let $R = \bigoplus_{\gamma \in \Gamma} R_\gamma$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:graded_ring_by_a_monoid}{graded ring}. An \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ideal_of_a_ring}{ideal} $I \subseteq R$ (left, right, or two-sided) is called \hldef{homogeneous} (or \hldef{graded}) if it satisfies any of the following equivalent conditions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $I$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ideal_generated_by_a_subset_of_a_ring}{generated by} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:graded_ring_by_a_monoid}{homogeneous elements}.
    \item For every $r \in I$, all homogeneous components of $r$ also belong to $I$.
    \item $I = \bigoplus_{\gamma \in \Gamma} (I \cap R_\gamma)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Local field] \label{definition:local_field}

A \hldef{local field} is a field $K$ with a nontrivial \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:absolute_value_on_a_field}{absolute value} $|\cdot|$ such that $K$ is \hyperrefIfExists{definition:locally_compact_topological_space}{locally compact}\CrefIfExists{definition:locally_compact_topological_space} under the \hyperrefIfExists{definition:topology_induced_by_a_norm_on_a_vector_space_over_a_field_with_absolute_value}{topology induced by $|\cdot|$}; we almost always treat a local field as a topological field with this topology. 

The local field $K$ is called \hldef{archimedean} if its absolute value is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:archimedean_absolute_value_on_a_field}{archimedean} and is called \hldef{non-archimedean} if its absolute value is non-archimedean.

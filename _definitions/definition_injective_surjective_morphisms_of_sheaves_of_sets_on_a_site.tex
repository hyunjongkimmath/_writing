\begin{definition} \label{definition:injective_surjective_morphisms_of_sheaves_of_sets_on_a_site}
Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendieck_topology_on_a_category_site_covering_sieve_topologically_generating_family}{site}. Let $\mathcal{F}$ and $\mathcal{G}$ be \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:sheaf_on_a_site}{sheaves} of sets on $\mathcal{C}$, and let $\phi: \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{G}$ be a morphism of sheaves.
\begin{itemize}
    \item The morphism $\phi$ is called \hldef{injective} if for every object $U$ in $\mathcal{C}$, the map of sets $\phi_U: \mathcal{F}(U) \to \mathcal{G}(U)$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:injective_surjective_bijective_map_of_sets}{injective}. Equivalently, $\phi$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:monomorphism_and_epimorphism_in_categories}{monomorphism} in the category $\Sh(\calC, \Sets)$ of sheaves on $\calC$ valued in sets. 

    \TODO{This needs some genuine fixing to be definable on a general site, not just one given by a pretopology}
    \item The morphism $\phi$ is called \hldef{surjective} if for every object $U$ in $\mathcal{C}$ and every section $s \in \mathcal{G}(U)$, there exists a covering $\{U_i \to U\}_{i \in I}$ in the topology of $\mathcal{C}$ such that for each $i$, the restriction $s|_{U_i}$ is in the image of $\phi_{U_i}: \mathcal{F}(U_i) \to \mathcal{G}(U_i)$. Equivalently, $\phi$ is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:monomorphism_and_epimorphism_in_categories}{epimorphism} in the category $\Sh(\calC, \Sets)$ of sheaves on $\calC$ valued in sets. 

\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

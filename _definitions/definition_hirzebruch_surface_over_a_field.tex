Let $k$ be a field and let $n \ge 0$ be an integer. Let $\mathbb{P}^1_k$ denote the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_space_of_dimension_n_over_a_scheme}{projective line} over $k$, equipped with its structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}$ and the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:twisting_sheaf_of_serre_of_degree_n_on_projective_space_over_a_scheme}{twisting sheaf} $\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}(n)$ (the line bundle of degree $n$). The \hldef{Hirzebruch surface of degree $n$}, denoted by \hl{$\mathbb{F}_n$} (or sometimes \hl{$\Sigma_n$}), is the projective bundle associated to the rank-2 \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraic_vector_bundle_over_a_scheme}{vector bundle} $\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1} \oplus \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}(n)$. That is,
$$\hlin{\mathbb{F}_n = \mathbb{P}(\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1} \oplus \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}(n)) = \operatorname{Proj}\left( \operatorname{Sym}^\bullet(\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1} \oplus \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}(n)) \right).}$$
The natural projection $\pi: \mathbb{F}_n \to \mathbb{P}^1$ gives $\mathbb{F}_n$ the structure of a $\mathbb{P}^1$-bundle over $\mathbb{P}^1$.
\end{definition}

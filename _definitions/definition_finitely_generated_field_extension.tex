\begin{definition}[Finitely generated field extension] \label{definition:finitely_generated_field_extension}
Let $E/F$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension}.
We say that $E/F$ is \hldef{finitely generated} if there exist finitely many elements $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n \in E$ such that \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field_generated_by_elements}{$E = F(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n)$}, where $F(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n)$ denotes the smallest subfield of $E$ containing $F$ and $\{\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n\}$.
\end{definition}

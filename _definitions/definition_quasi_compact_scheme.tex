\begin{definition}[Quasi-Compact Scheme] \label{definition:quasi_compact_scheme}
    A \hldef{quasi-compact scheme} $X$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme} such that the underlying \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:topological_space}{topological space} of $X$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:compact_topological_space}{quasi-compact}. That is, every open cover of $X$ admits a finite subcover.

    Equivalently, $X$ is quasi-compact if it can be covered by finitely many \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:affine_open_subscheme_of_a_scheme}{affine open subschemes} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:affine_scheme}{$\Spec A_i$}.
\end{definition}

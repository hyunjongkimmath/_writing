\begin{definition} \label{definition:etale_O_X_algebra_over_a_scheme}
    Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme} and let $A$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quasi_coherent_sheaf_on_a_general_scheme}{quasi-coherent sheaf} of \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebra_over_a_sheaf_of_rings_on_a_site}{$\mathcal{O}_X$}-algebras (equivalently, a quasi-coherent sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-algebras). We say that $A$ is an \hldef{étale $X$-algebra} if the associated morphism $f \colon \underline{\operatorname{Spec}} A \to X$ from the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:relative_spectrum_of_a_quasi_coherent_sheaf_of_O_algebras_on_a_scheme}{relative spectrum of $A$} is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:etale_morphism_of_schemes}{étale morphism of schemes}.
    % Equivalently, if $X = \operatorname{Spec} R$ is affine and $A$ corresponds to an $R$-algebra $B$, then $A$ is an étale $X$-algebra if and only if the ring homomorphism $R \to B$ is étale, meaning $B$ is flat over $R$ and the sheaf of relative differentials $\Omega^1_{B/R} = 0$.
\end{definition}

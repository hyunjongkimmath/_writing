\begin{definition}[Separating transcendence basis] \label{definition:separating_transcendence_basis_of_a_field_extension}
Let $E/F$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension}, and let $T \subseteq E$ be a transcendence basis of $E$ over $F$.
We say that $T$ is a \hldef{separating transcendence basis} if the algebraic extension $E/F(T)$\CrefIfExists{definition:field_generated_by_elements} is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:separable_algebraic_field_extension}{separable}, i.e., every element of $E$ is separable over $F(T)$ (meaning that its \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraic_element_minimal_polynomial_of_an_algebraic_element_algebraic_field_extension_transcendental_element_field_extension}{minimal polynomial} over $F(T)$ has no repeated roots in an algebraic closure).
\end{definition}

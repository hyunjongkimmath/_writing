\begin{definition} \label{definition:sylow_p_subgroup_of_a_finite_group}
Let $G$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:countable_finite_uncountable_sets}{finite} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group}{group} of order $p^n m$, where $p$ is a prime number and $p$ does not divide $m$.
A \hldef{Sylow $p$-subgroup} of $G$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subgroup_of_a_group}{subgroup} of order $p^n$.

The set of all Sylow $p$-subgroups of a group $G$ is denoted by \hl{$\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)$}. The number of Sylow $p$-subgroups is denoted by \hl{$n_p(G)$} or simply \hl{$n_p$}. 
\end{definition}

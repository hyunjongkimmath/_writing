\begin{definition} \label{definition:radical_of_an_ideal_of_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring} and let $I$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ideal_of_a_ring}{ideal of $R$}. The \hldef{radical of $I$}, denoted by \hl{$\sqrt{I}$} or \hl{$\operatorname{rad}(I)$}, is the set of all elements $x \in R$ such that some positive integer power of $x$ belongs to $I$. That is,
$$\sqrt{I} = \{x \in R \mid \exists n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1} \text{ such that } x^n \in I\}.$$
The radical $\sqrt{I}$ is itself an ideal of $R$ containing $I$.
\end{definition}

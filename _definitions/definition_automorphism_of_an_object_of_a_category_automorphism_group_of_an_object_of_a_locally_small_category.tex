\begin{definition} \label{definition:automorphism_of_an_object_of_a_category_automorphism_group_of_an_object_of_a_locally_small_category}
Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:category}{category} and $X$ be an object of $\mathcal{C}$.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item An \hldef{automorphism of $X$} is a morphism $f: X \to X$ that is an isomorphism. That is, there exists a morphism $g: X \to X$ such that $f \circ g = \operatorname{id}_X$ and $g \circ f = \operatorname{id}_X$.

    \item Assume that $\mathcal{C}$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:locally_small_category}{locally small category}. The \hldef{automorphism group of $X$}, denoted \hl{$\operatorname{Aut}_{\mathcal{C}}(X)$}, is the set of all automorphisms of $X$. It is indeed a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:group}{group}\CrefIfExists{proposition:automorphism_group_of_an_object_of_a_locally_small_category_is_indeed_a_group} and the group operation is the composition of morphisms in $\mathcal{C}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}


\begin{definition} \label{definition:finitely_generated_algebra_over_a_not_necessarily_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{(not-necessarily commutative) ring} and let $A$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebra_of_a_ring}{$R$-algebra}. 
We say that $A$ is a \hldef{finitely generated $R$-algebra} or synoymously that the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{ring homomorphism} $R \to A$ is \hldef{of finite type} if there exists a finite subset $\{a_1, \dots, a_n\} \subseteq A$ such that \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subalgebra_of_an_algebra_over_a_not_necessarily_commutative_ring_generated_by_a_subset}{$R$-subalgebra of $A$ generated by} $\{a_1, \dots, a_n\}$ is equal to $A$. Equivalently, every element of $A$ can be expressed, using a finite composition of ring operations and $R$-linear combinations, from the generators $\{a_1, \dots, a_n\}$.
\end{definition}

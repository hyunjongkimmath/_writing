\begin{definition} \label{definition:pre_logarithmic_structure_and_logarithmic_structure_on_a_scheme_and_log_scheme}
Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme} with structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$. A \hldef{pre-logarithmic structure on $X$} is a sheaf of commutative \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:monoid}{monoids} $M$ and a morphism of monoids $\alpha: M \to \mathcal{O}_X$ where $\mathcal{O}_X$ is viewed as a monoid under multiplication. A \hldef{logarithmic structure} (log structure) is a pre-log structure $(M,\alpha)$ inducing an isomorphism $\alpha^{-1}(\mathcal{O}_X^\times) \cong \mathcal{O}_X^\times$. The pair $(X,M)$ is called a \hldef{log scheme}.  
\end{definition}

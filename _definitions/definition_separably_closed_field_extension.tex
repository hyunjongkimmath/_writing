\begin{definition}\label{definition:separably_closed_field_extension}
Let $K$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:field}{field}. A \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension} $L/K$ is called a \hldef{separable closure of $K$} if the following hold:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The extension $L/K$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:separable_algebraic_field_extension}{separable algebraic} (i.e., every $a \in L$ is separable over $K$).
    \item The field $L$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:separably_closed_field}{separably closed}.
\end{enumerate}
One often writes a separable closure of $K$ by \hl{$K^{\text{sep}}$}. Some also write separable closures of $K$ by \hl{$\overline{K}$}, which may conflict with the common notation for algebraic closures of $K$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:reduced_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{ring}. We say that $R$ is a \hldef{reduced ring} if the only \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:nilpotent_element_of_a_ring}{nilpotent element} of $R$ is the zero element. If $R$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative}, this is equivalent to the condition that the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:nilradical_of_a_commutative_ring}{nilradical} of $R$ is the zero ideal, i.e., $\mathfrak{N}(R) = (0)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:nondegenerate_quadratic_form_on_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring}. Let $q: M \to R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quadratic_formm_on_a_module_over_a_commutative_ring}{quadratic form} on a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:module_of_a_ring}{module} over $R$.

The quadratic form $q$ is called \hldef{\emph{non-degenerate}} if $B_q(x,-) = 0$ implies $x = 0$.
\TextIfExists{definition:nondegenerate_quadratic_form_on_a_module_over_an_involution_ring}{Equivalently, $q$ is nondegenerate if it is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:nondegenerate_quadratic_form_on_a_module_over_an_involution_ring}{nondegenerate} when considered as a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:quadratic_form_over_a_ring_with_involution}{quadratic form} on the module $M$ where $R$ is considered as an involution ring with the identity map.}
\end{definition}

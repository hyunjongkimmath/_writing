\begin{definition} \label{definition:normal_crossings_effective_Q_divisor_on_a_regular_scheme}
Let $X$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:regular_at_a_point_for_a_scheme_regular_scheme_nonsingular_scheme}{regular scheme}, and let $D$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:effective_Q_divisor_on_a_scheme}{effective $\mathbb{Q}$-divisor on $X$}.
\begin{itemize}
    \item The pair $(X, D)$ has \hldef{simple normal crossings at a point $x \in X$} if the local ring $\mathcal{O}_{X,x}$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:regular_ring}{regular} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:local_ring}{local ring} and the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:support_of_a_Q_divisor_on_a_scheme}{support of $D$} near $x$ is defined by an equation of the form $x_1^{a_1} \cdots x_r^{a_r} = 0$ (where the exponents $a_i$ are positive rational numbers), such that $\{x_1, \dots, x_n\}$ can be extended to a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:regular_system_of_parameters_of_a_noetherian_local_ring}{regular system of parameters} $\{x_1, \dots, x_d\}$ for the local ring $\mathcal{O}_{X,x}$ (where $d = \dim \mathcal{O}_{X,x}$).
    \item The pair $(X, D)$ has \hldef{simple normal crossings} (globally) if it has simple normal crossings at every point $x \in X$. This implies that the irreducible components of the support of $D$ are regular schemes that intersect transversally.
    \item The pair $(X, D)$ has \hldef{normal crossings} if it is locally simple normal crossings in the \'etale topology. More explicitly, for every point $x \in X$, there exists an Ã©tale neighborhood $f: U \to X$ of $x$ such that the pullback pair $(U, f^*D)$ has simple normal crossings on $U$.

\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

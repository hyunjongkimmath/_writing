\begin{definition} \label{definition:effective_Q_divisor_on_a_scheme}
Let $X$ be an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:integral_scheme}{integral scheme}. A $\mathbb{Q}$-divisor on $X$ given by $D = \sum q_i Y_i$, where the $Y_i$ are prime divisors and the coefficients $q_i$ are rational numbers, with only finitely many $q_i$ non-zero, is called \hldef{effective} if $q_i \geq 0$ for all $i$.
\end{definition}

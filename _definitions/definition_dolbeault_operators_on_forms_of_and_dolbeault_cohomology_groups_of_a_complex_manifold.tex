
\begin{definition}[Dolbeault operators on forms on a complex manifold] \label{definition:dolbeault_operators_on_forms_of_and_dolbeault_cohomology_groups_of_a_complex_manifold}
Let $X$ be a complex manifold.

The Dolbeault operators $\partial$ and $\overline{\partial}$ are the first-order differential operators defined as the compositions

$$\hlin{\partial: \Omega^{p,q}(X) \xrightarrow{d} \Omega^{p+q+1}(X; \bbC) \xrightarrow{\pi^{p+1,q}} \Omega^{p+1,q}(X), \quad \overline{\partial}: \Omega^{p,q}(X) \xrightarrow{d} \Omega^{p+q+1}(X; \bbC) \xrightarrow{\pi^{p,q+1}} \Omega^{p,q+1}(X)}$$
where \(\pi^{p+1,q}\) and \(\pi^{p,q+1}\) denotes projection onto forms of type \((p+1,q)\) and \((p,q+1)\) respectively. In particular, the exterior derivative $d: \Omega^{p,q}(X) \to \Omega^{p+q+1}(X; \mathbb{C})$ decomposes uniquely as
$$d = \partial + \overline{\partial}.$$
The Dolbeault operators satisfy
$$ \partial^2 = 0, \quad \overline{\partial}^2 = 0, \quad \partial \circ \overline{\partial} + \overline{\partial} \circ \partial = 0.  $$

In particular, \((\Omega^{p,\bullet}(X), \overline{\partial})\) is a cochain complex for each fixed \(p\). The \hldef{Dolbeault cohomology groups} is then defined as the cohomology of this complex:
$$ \hlin{ H^{p,q}_{\overline{\partial}}(X) := \frac{\ker \left(\overline{\partial} : \Omega^{p,q}(X) \to \Omega^{p,q+1}(X)\right)}{\operatorname{im} \left(\overline{\partial} : \Omega^{p,q-1}(X) \to \Omega^{p,q}(X)\right)}.  } $$
\end{definition}

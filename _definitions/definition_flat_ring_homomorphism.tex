\begin{definition}[Flat ring homomorphism] \label{definition:flat_ring_homomorphism}
    \TODO{flat module}
    Let $R$ and $S$ be \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{(not necessarily commutative) rings}, and let $\varphi: R \to S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{ring homomorphism}.
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item We may say that $S$ is \hldef{flat as a left module} or that $\varphi$ is \hldef{flat (as a left module)} if \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:tensor_product_of_bimodules_of_rings}{$- \otimes_R S: \mathrm{Mod}_R \to \mathrm{Mod}_S$} is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:exact_functor_between_abelian_categories}{exact}.
        \item We may say that $S$ is \hldef{flat as a right module} or that $\varphi$ is \hldef{flat (as a right module)} if \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:tensor_product_of_bimodules_of_rings}{$S \otimes_R -: {}_{R} \mathrm{Mod}_R \to {}_{S} \mathrm{Mod}$} is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:exact_functor_between_abelian_categories}{exact}.
    \end{enumerate}
    These two notions of flatness are different in general. However, if $\varphi$ equips $S$ with the structure of an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebra_of_a_ring}{$R$-algebra}, then these two notions of flatness coincide. In this case, we may speak of the notion of \hldef{flatness of $R$-algebras} and say that $S$ is \hldef{flat over $R$}.

    If $S$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:faithfully_flat_left_right_module_over_a_ring}{faithfully flat} as a left/right $R$-module, then we may say that $\varphi$ is \hldef{faithfully flat (as a left/right $R$-module)}.
\end{definition}

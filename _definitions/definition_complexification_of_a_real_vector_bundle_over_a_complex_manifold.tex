\begin{definition}[Complexified vector bundle of a real vector bundle on a complex manifold] \label{definition:complexification_of_a_real_vector_bundle_over_a_complex_manifold}

    \TODO{TODO: define a complex vector bundle}
Let $M$ be a complex manifold of complex dimension $n$, that is, a \hyperrefIfExists{definition:topological_manifold}{real} $2n$-dimensional \hyperrefIfExists{definition:C_k_manifold}{smooth manifold} equipped with an atlas whose transition maps are holomorphic. Let $E \to M$ be a \hyperrefIfExists{definition:C_k_vector_bundle_on_C_k_manifold}{real vector bundle} of real rank $r$ over the smooth manifold $M$.

The \hldef{complexified vector bundle of $E$} is the complex vector bundle \hl{$E^\mathbb{C} \to M$} defined by

$$ E^\mathbb{C} := E \otimes_{\mathbb{R}} \mathbb{C}, $$

where $E^\mathbb{C}_x = E_x \otimes_{\mathbb{R}} \mathbb{C}$ is the complexification of the real vector space $E_x$ at each point $x \in M$. It is a complex vector bundle of complex rank $r$ over $M$.

In particular, we may speak of the \hldef{complexified tangent bundle} \hl{$T_{\mathbb{C}}X$} and the \hldef{complexified cotangent bundle} \hl{$T_{\mathbb{C}}^* X$} of $X$.

\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Galois Extension] \label{definition:galois_extension_of_fields}
An extension $L/K$ is called a \hldef{Galois extension} if it is both a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:splitting_field_of_polynomials_over_a_field_normal_field_extension}{normal extension} and a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:separable_algebraic_field_extension}{separable algebraic extension}. Its \hldef{Galois group}, usually denoted by \hl{$\Gal(L/K)$}, is defined to be the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:automorphism_group_of_a_field_extension}{automorphism group $\operatorname{Aut}(L/K)$}.
\end{definition}

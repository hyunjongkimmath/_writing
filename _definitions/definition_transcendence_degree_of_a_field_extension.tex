\begin{definition}[Transcendence degree] \label{definition:transcendence_degree_of_a_field_extension}
Let $E/F$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension}. 
The \hldef{transcendence degree of $E$ over $F$}, denoted \hl{$\operatorname{tr.deg}_F(E)$} or \hl{$\operatorname{trdeg}_F(E)$}, is defined as the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:cardinal_number_and_cardinality_of_a_set}{cardinality} of any \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:transcendence_basis_of_a_field_extension}{transcendence basis} of $E$ over $F$. By a classical result, all transcendence bases of $E$ over $F$ have the same cardinality, so the transcendence degree is well-defined. 

If $E/F$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:algebraic_element_minimal_polynomial_of_an_algebraic_element_algebraic_field_extension_transcendental_element_field_extension}{algebraic}, then $\operatorname{tr.deg}_F(E) = 0$. If no transcendence basis is finite, then $\operatorname{tr.deg}_F(E)$ is an infinite cardinal.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:nilradical_of_a_commutative_ring}
Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:commutative_ring}{commutative ring}. The \hldef{nilradical of $R$}, denoted by \hl{$\mathfrak{N}(R)$}, is the subset of $R$ consisting of all \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:nilpotent_element_of_a_ring}{nilpotent elements}. It forms an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ideal_of_a_ring}{ideal of $R$} and is equal to the intersection of all \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:prime_and_maximal_ideal_of_a_ring}{prime ideals} of $R$. Equivalently, the nilradical of $R$ is the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:radical_of_an_ideal_of_a_commutative_ring}{radical} of the zero ideal and hence may be denoted by \hl{$\sqrt{(0)}$}.
\end{definition}

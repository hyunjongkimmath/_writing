\begin{definition} \label{definition:absorbing_zero_positive_cone_in_a_monoid}
Let $\Gamma$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:monoid}{monoid} with an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:absorbing_zero_of_a_monoid}{absorbing zero} $0 \in \Gamma$. A \hldef{positive cone in $\Gamma$} is a submonoid $\Gamma_+ \subseteq \Gamma$ such that:
    \TODO{submonoid}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $0 \in \Gamma_+$ and the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:left_right_identity_element_of_a_binary_operation}{identity element} $e \in \Gamma_+$ (if $0 \neq e$).
    \item $\Gamma_+$ generates $\Gamma$ as a group (if $\Gamma$ is cancellative) or satisfies some appropriate generating condition relevant to the context.
    \item Often, "positive" implies that the only invertible element in $\Gamma_+$ is the identity, or that there is a partial order $\le$ on $\Gamma$ such that $\gamma \in \Gamma_+ \iff \gamma \ge e$.
\end{enumerate}
(Note: In the context of graded rings, one typically requires that $\Gamma$ is a cancellative monoid and the grading is supported on $\Gamma_+$, but the "irrelevant ideal" definition below only strictly requires distinguishing "zero" degrees from "positive" degrees).
\end{definition}

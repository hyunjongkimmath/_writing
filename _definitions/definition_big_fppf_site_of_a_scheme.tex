\begin{definition} \label{definition:big_fppf_site_of_a_scheme}
Let $S$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:scheme}{scheme}. The \hldef{big fppf site of $S$}, denoted \hl{$(\mathit{Sch}/S)_{fppf}$}, is the following \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendieck_topology_on_a_category_site_covering_sieve_topologically_generating_family}{site}:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The underlying category is the category whose \begin{enumerate}
        \item objects are schemes $U$ equipped with a morphism $U \to S$, and 
        \item morphisms are $S$-morphisms of schemes. 
    \end{enumerate}
    \item The \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendieck_topology_on_a_category_site_covering_sieve_topologically_generating_family}{Grothendieck topology} is the one \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:grothendieck_topology_generated_by_a_pretopology}{generated by} the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:basis_and_grothendieck_pretopology_for_a_grothendieck_topology_on_a_category}{pretopology} for which a covering is a family of morphisms $\{ f_i: U_i \to U \}$ such that each $U_i \to U$ is \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:flat_morphism_of_schemes}{flat}, \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:locally_of_finite_presentation_finite_presentation_morphism_of_schemes}{locally of finite presentation}, and jointly surjective, i.e. $\bigcup_{i} f_i(U_i) = U$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}


\begin{definition} \label{definition:extension_of_a_ring}
    Let $R$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring}{not necessarily commutative ring}. A \hldef{extension ring of $R$} is an $R$-algebra $A$ such that the ring homomorphism $R \to A$ is an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:injective_surjective_bijective_map_of_sets}{injection}. We also say that $A/R$ is an \hldef{extension of rings}.

    In this case, the image of $R \to A$ is a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:subring_of_a_ring}{subring} of $A$ \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:ring_homomorphism}{isomorphic} to $R$; we often identify $R$ with this image to consider $R$ as a subring of $A$.
\end{definition}

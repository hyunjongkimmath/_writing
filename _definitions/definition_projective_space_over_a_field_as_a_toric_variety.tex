\begin{definition} \label{definition:projective_space_over_a_field_as_a_toric_variety}
The \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:projective_space_of_dimension_n_over_a_scheme}{projective space $\mathbb{P}^n_k$} is a complete toric variety. The torus $T = (\mathbb{G}_m)^n$ acts on $\mathbb{P}^n$ via:
$$(t_1, \dots, t_n) \cdot [x_0 : x_1 : \dots : x_n] = [x_0 : t_1 x_1 : \dots : t_n x_n].$$
The \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{theorem:fans_of_a_real_fin_dim_vector_space_correspond_to_normal_separated_toric_varieties_over_an_algebraicaly_closed_field}{corresponding} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:fan_in_a_finite_dimensional_real_vector_space}{fan} $\Sigma_{\mathbb{P}^n}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ is generated by the vectors $v_0, v_1, \dots, v_n$, where $\{v_1, \dots, v_n\} = \{e_1, \dots, e_n\}$ is the standard basis and $v_0 = -(e_1 + \dots + e_n)$. The maximal cones are those generated by any proper subset of $\{v_0, \dots, v_n\}$ of size $n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition} \label{definition:embedding_dimension_of_a_noetherian_local_ring_and_a_regular_local_ring}
Let $(A, \mathfrak{m})$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:noetherian_ring}{Noetherian} \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:local_ring}{local ring}. The \hldef{embedding dimension of $A$} is the dimension of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:zariski_cotangent_space_of_tangent_space_of_a_local_ring}{cotangent space $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$} as a vector space over the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:residue_field_of_a_ring_at_a_point}{residue field} $k = A/\mathfrak{m}$. The ring $A$ is called a \hldef{regular local ring} if its global dimension is finite, or equivalently, if its embedding dimension equals its \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:krull_dimension_of_a_commutative_ring}{Krull dimension}:
$$\dim_k (\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2) = \dim(A).$$
\end{definition}

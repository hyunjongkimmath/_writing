
\begin{definition}[Field Generated by Elements] \label{definition:field_generated_by_elements}
Let $L/K$ be a \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:extension_of_a_field}{field extension} and let $S \subseteq L$ be a subset. The \hldef{field generated by $S$ over $K$} is the smallest subfield of $L$ that contains $K$ and $S$. Equivalently, it is the intersection of all subfields of $L$ containing $K \cup S$. This field is denoted by \hl{$K(S)$}.

In the special case where $S = \{x_1, \dots, x_n\}$ is finite, one writes  
\hl{$K(x_1, \dots, x_n)$}.  
If $n=1$, then $K(x)$ is called a \hldef{simple field extension}.
\end{definition}

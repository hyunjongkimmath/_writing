\begin{convention} \label{convention:objects_of_an_abelian_category_can_be_regarded_as_objects_of_the_derived_category}
    Given an \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:derived_category_of_an_abelian_category}{abelian category} $A$, any object $M$ of $A$ may be identified with an object of the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:homotopy_category_of_chain_complexes_of_an_additive_category}{homotopy category $K(A)$} and the \CrefAndHyperrefIfExist{definition:derived_category_of_an_abelian_category}{derived category $D(A)$} --- simply take the chain complex concentrated at degree $0$ with the object $M$. In fact, this derived object can often be regarded as an object of ``nice'' enough subcategories of $D(A)$, such as the bounded derived categories $D^+(A)$, $D^-(A)$, $D^b(A)$.
\end{convention}
